Start your own sourdough.

Starting your own sourdough is not that hard as you might think. If you have a friend you can ask them for a starter.

If not,there's a couple of ways of doing it but this way has worked well for me.


Psst, I've posted a video down below if you want some inspiration...

Do like this.

  1. 1.
    Pour 200 grams of water (about 37-40 degrees celsius) in a jar.
  2. 2.
    Add 200 grams of flour and stir until you have a porridge like paste.
    I like to use wholegrain flour for starting a sourdough because it's rich with microorganisms and nutrition which will help you sourdough to get started.
  3. 3.
    Put in in a warm place, about 24 degrees celsius, like over the fridge. After about 12- 24 hours your sourdough should be alive and bubbeling. Of not, chances are that your flour is to old.
